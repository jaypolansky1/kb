---

title: How to Change your Pace Goal

---

Your pace goal is the target 500m split that drives everything on your dashboard — the color-coded zones, the goal line on your charts, and even your AI coach's advice. Here's how to change it and what happens when you do.

#### Where to find the pace goal selector

At the top of your dashboard you'll see a dropdown showing your current goal (for example, 2:00). Click it to open the full list of available goals, ranging from 1:25 to 2:30 per 500m in 5-second steps.

#### Changing your goal

Just pick a new pace from the dropdown. The change saves instantly to your account — no confirmation needed — and applies everywhere, on every device you log in from.

#### What changes when you update your goal

Every zone and chart on your dashboard re-shades immediately based on your new target:

* In Zone (green) — workouts at or faster than your goal pace
* Near Miss (yellow) — workouts within 5 seconds of your goal
* Building (orange) — workouts more than 5 seconds over your goal

You'll also see:

* The dashed goal line on your pace progression chart moves to the new pace, and the shaded goal zone follows it
* Your In Zone count and streaks recalculate against the new target
* Workout cards re-label — a session that was a "Near Miss" against 2:00 might show "In Zone" against 2:05
* Your AI coach tailors its advice to the new target pace

#### Choosing the right goal

A good pace goal is one you hit in roughly a third to half of your steady sessions. If everything shows green, it's time to move the goal down. If almost everything is orange, set a friendlier target and work your way back — the zones are most useful when they show a mix.
